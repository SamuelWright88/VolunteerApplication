Welcome to Privacy & Screening

This module explains how we protect personal information and ensure safe, suitable people are working within the church.

  • understand privacy responsibilities
  • learn how information must be handled
  • understand screening and Blue Card requirements
  • apply safe practices in real situations

Privacy and screening protect individuals, families and the church.

Why Privacy Matters

Churches collect and hold personal and sensitive information.

  • names, contact details and family information
  • medical information
  • attendance and participation records
  • child safety or incident records

This information must be handled carefully, respectfully and lawfully.

Your Responsibilities

As a volunteer, you must:

  • keep personal information confidential
  • only access information needed for your role
  • use information only for its intended purpose
  • store and handle information securely
  • report any misuse or breach of information

Privacy is a responsibility, not just a system.

What You Must Not Do

  • share personal information with others unnecessarily
  • discuss private matters casually with others
  • post photos or details without permission
  • store information on personal devices without approval
  • access records you do not need
If in doubt — don’t share it.

Photos and Media

Photos and videos must be handled carefully.

  • only take photos in line with church procedures
  • respect opt-out and consent requirements
  • do not post identifiable images without approval
  • consider safety before sharing any content

Images can expose identity and location, especially for children.

Handling Sensitive Information

Sensitive information requires extra care.

  • medical conditions
  • child safety concerns
  • family or custody matters

This information must only be shared with authorised people when necessary.

Screening and Blue Cards

Screening ensures safe and suitable people work with children.

  • all adults working with children must hold a valid Blue Card
  • screening must be completed before starting
  • clearances must remain current

Screening is a legal requirement and a safety measure.

Ongoing Suitability

Screening is not a one-time check.

  • ongoing supervision and accountability apply
  • concerns about behaviour must be raised
  • changes in circumstances may affect suitability

Safe people are supported, supervised and accountable.

Data Security

Information must be stored and handled securely.

  • use approved systems and storage
  • do not leave records unattended
  • log out of shared devices
  • protect passwords and access

Security failures can expose sensitive information.

When a Breach Happens

If information is shared incorrectly or lost:

  • report it immediately
  • do not attempt to hide the issue
  • follow church procedures

Quick action reduces risk and harm.
