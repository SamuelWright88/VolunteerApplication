Family Church Volunteer Induction

Module 1: Vision, Mission & Core Values

Welcome to Family Church

This module introduces the vision, mission and core values that shape who we are and how we serve.

You will:

  • learn the overall vision of the church
  • understand the mission that drives our ministry
  • be introduced to the core values that guide our culture and conduct

These foundations help every volunteer understand not just what we do, but why we do it.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values; Code of Conduct

Our Vision

Our vision is:

...to help expand the Kingdom of Jesus Christ on earth by creating Bible-based, contemporary local churches and ministries.

This vision gives direction to our church life, ministry and service.

It reminds us that our work is not only about one event, one team or one location. It is about seeing the Kingdom of Jesus Christ extended on earth through healthy local churches and ministries.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values

Our Mission

Our mission is:

...to exalt Jesus Christ in all that we think, say and do, and to extend His influence throughout the world.

This mission applies to every person serving in the church.

As volunteers, we help carry this mission through the way we speak, serve, welcome, lead, support and represent the church.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values

How We Outwork Our Mission

The mission is expressed in practical ways through the life of the church. This includes:

  • proclaiming Christ’s Gospel with the expectation that supernatural signs will follow as the normal New Testament pattern
  • planting Christ-centred churches that are autonomous, interdependent and self-propagating
  • promoting Christ-glorifying Christian communities that outwork the miraculous and character-transforming ministry of the Holy Spirit
  • producing Christ-following disciples who seek to obey the Great Commandment and the Great Commission

These statements show that our mission is both spiritual and practical. It affects preaching, discipleship, leadership, community life and outreach.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values

Why Core Values Matter

Core values shape the culture of the church.

They help guide:

  • how we think
  • how we make decisions
  • how we relate to others
  • how we serve in ministry

Values are not just ideas. They are meant to be visible in our attitudes, actions and ministry practice.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values; Code of Conduct

Core Value: Word Based

We believe that God will work through His living, authoritative and relevant Bible.

We show this value by:

  • ensuring that all theology, ministry practices and organisational policies and processes are Biblically based
  • continually reforming ourselves, our local churches and our movement by aligning ourselves to God’s Word

This means that our ministry is not built around personal preference or changing culture alone. God’s Word remains our foundation.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values

Core Values: Multiplying Churches & Interdependent Ministries

We believe in multiplication, connection and accountability.

  • Multiplying Churches: we believe in the God-inspired strategy of covering the earth with churches
  • Interdependent Ministries: we believe in connected, cooperative and accountable ministers and churches

This means we are not isolated. We are part of a wider movement and we serve with a spirit of cooperation, responsibility and shared mission.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values

Core Values: New Creation Message & Gospel of Grace

We believe in the life-changing power of Christ’s finished work.

  • New Creation Message: we teach rest in the finished work of Christ and the reality of Christ in us through the Holy Spirit
  • Gospel of Grace: we maintain the conviction that Jesus Christ can save and transform anyone who places full trust in Him

These values remind us that Christian service flows from identity in Christ, not from performance, pride or pressure.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values

Core Values: Stable Spirituality & Contemporary Ministry

We seek both Biblical stability and ministry relevance.

  • Stable Spirituality: we value both the Word of God and the Spirit of God as essential for growth and stability
  • Contemporary Ministry: we aim to be creative and culturally relevant while remaining doctrinally pure

This means we serve in ways that are wise, current and effective, while remaining grounded in truth.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values

Core Values: Relationships, Faith & Service

Several of our values strongly shape volunteer culture:

  • Harmonious Relationships: we pursue unity, integrity, love and strong relationships
  • Dynamic Faith: we believe in forward-moving and daring faith in response to the Holy Spirit’s leading
  • Selfless Service: we value sacrificial servanthood as a way of life

These values affect how we work with leaders, teams, families, children, young people and the wider church community.

Referenced documents: Church Vision, Mission & Core Values; Code of Conduct

What This Means for You as a Volunteer

As a volunteer, these values should be reflected in the way you serve.

  • speak and act in ways that honour Jesus Christ
  • treat people with dignity, patience and respect
  • serve with humility, faith and reliability
  • support unity and healthy relationships
  • represent the church well in all settings

Your conduct helps strengthen trust, culture and witness within the church.
