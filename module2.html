Family Church Volunteer Induction

Module 2: Code of Conduct

Welcome to the Code of Conduct

This module explains the standards of behaviour expected of all church leaders, staff and volunteers who represent Family Church.

You will:

  • understand expected conduct and boundaries
  • learn what safe, respectful behaviour looks like
  • review privacy, social media and reporting expectations
  • apply the policy to realistic situations

These expectations help protect children, young people, vulnerable adults, volunteers and the church. They also reduce the risk of harm, inappropriate behaviour and allegations.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct; Child Safe Policy

Who the Code Applies To

The Code of Conduct applies to all church leaders, staff and volunteers who represent Cairns Family Church, particularly those who work with children, young people and vulnerable adults.

The code applies across:

  • official church services
  • official church programs
  • ministry activities on or off site
  • personal conduct where you are representing the church

You represent the church in both public and private settings connected to your role.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct

You Must

The policy sets out clear expectations for volunteers and team members.

  • treat all people, including children, with respect
  • be a positive role model to children
  • set clear boundaries about appropriate behaviour
  • have another adult present or in sight when doing anything one-on-one with a child
  • raise concerns or issues with your team leader as soon as possible
  • set safe boundaries so children can have fun without hurting themselves or others
  • obtain approval before showing anything rated other than G
  • manage risk around physical activity

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct

You Must Not

Some actions are clearly prohibited.

  • be alone with a child
  • engage in rough physical activities
  • touch a child inappropriately
  • use inappropriate language such as swearing
  • yell at children
  • develop a “special” relationship that could be seen as favouritism
  • do personal tasks for a child that they can do for themselves, such as changing clothes
Boundaries protect children and they also protect volunteers.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct

Behaviour and Role Modelling

Volunteers are expected to conduct themselves in a way that is consistent with the values of the church.

  • be mindful of your behaviour, actions, language and relationships
  • be transparent in your actions and whereabouts
  • challenge unacceptable behaviour
  • report allegations or suspicions of abuse
  • avoid behaviour that shames, humiliates or degrades anyone

Children and families learn as much from what volunteers do as from what they say.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct; Child Safe Policy

Language and Communication

The language you use should build worth, dignity and significance.

  • use language that is encouraging and appropriate
  • do not use offensive, insulting or discriminatory language
  • do not use put downs, sexist language or “in jokes” that exclude others
  • keep a calm tone when speaking with children
  • where private conversations are necessary, remain visible to another adult

Humour must never come at the expense of another person’s safety, dignity or sense of belonging.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct

Physical Contact and Personal Boundaries

Physical contact must always be appropriate, safe and respectful.

  • physical contact should be open, not secretive
  • it should be with the child’s permission where appropriate
  • it should take account of the child’s age and development
  • it must never be in the area normally covered by swimwear
  • it must never be sexual, coercive, threatening or humiliating
  • physical restraint is only appropriate to protect someone from harm

The policy also states that volunteers must not be alone with a child and must not enter sleeping spaces, toilet areas or change rooms of the opposite biological sex at church event locations, including homes.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct

Cultural Safety and Inclusion

The church expects volunteers to treat all children, young people and adults with respect.

  • respect people regardless of race, ethnicity, gender, language, religion, opinion, origin, property or disability
  • respect cultural differences
  • help create a welcoming, inclusive and safe environment
  • do not ridicule other cultures, traditions or religions
  • do not exclude anyone because of disability or additional needs

Inclusion is part of child safety and part of Christian conduct.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct; Child Safe Policy

Duty of Care and Safe Practice

Duty of care means taking reasonable care to protect children from reasonably foreseeable harm.

This includes:

  • active and appropriate supervision
  • following sign in and sign out procedures
  • maintaining safe environments
  • following procedures for illness, first aid, toileting and emergencies
  • ensuring youth transport and off-site activities are carried out safely

Your behaviour with children must always be regulated by this duty of care.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct; Child Safe Policy

Privacy and Social Media

Volunteers must respect the privacy of everyone who attends or interacts with the church.

  • keep child protection information confidential
  • do not spread rumours or allegations
  • use information only for the purpose it was collected
  • do not share personal or confidential information online
  • do not “friend” participants under 18 on personal social media
  • only post content you would be comfortable for a parent or carer to see
  • follow opt-out and photo/video restrictions

You represent the church online as well as offline.

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct; Privacy Policy

Reporting Concerns and Complaints

Concerns must be acted on promptly.

  • reasonable suspicion is enough to act — proof is not required
  • if a child discloses harm, do not promise secrecy
  • reassure the child that it is not their fault
  • do not ask probing questions
  • report the matter as soon as possible in line with policy
  • complete the required record of report form
  • non-child-safety complaints and feedback must also be taken seriously and responded to appropriately

Referenced policies: Code of Conduct; Child Safe Policy

Breach of the Code

A breach of the Code of Conduct is taken seriously.

Depending on the breach, outcomes may include:

  • further training
  • mentoring for accountability and support
  • stepping down from a position
  • being asked to leave the church
  • termination of employment
  • referral of illegal activity to authorities

The policy also notes that transparency and integrity may require a level of disclosure to relevant people or groups.

Key Takeaways

  • the Code of Conduct applies to all volunteers representing the church
  • respect, clear boundaries and safe supervision are essential
  • language, physical contact and relationships must remain appropriate
  • privacy and social media must be handled carefully
  • concerns, disclosures and complaints must be acted on promptly
  • breaches of the code can lead to serious consequences

Safe ministry depends on consistent conduct.
